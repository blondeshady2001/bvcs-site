ARTICLE I - NAME

The name of this organization, incorporated under the laws of Pennsylvania as a corporation not for profit, shall be the Beaver Valley Choral Society.

ARTICLE II - PURPOSE

The purposes for which said corporation as formed are:

    a) To promote a sense of community and civic responsibility through the discipline of music and to perform in a variety of concert settings;
    b) To provide scholarships and other forms of financial assistance to undergraduate and/or graduate music student(s) of an accredited college or university. This scholarship shall be irrevocably named "The Philip H. Inman Excellence in Choral Conducting Arts Scholarship".
    c) To provide an atmosphere in which to develop through music education an artistic sense of ensemble singing by expanding artistic discipline, concentration and commitment in order to perform challenging and rewarding works from select repertoire for the public;
    d) Such other lawful purposes for which non-profit charitable corporations may perform.

ARTICLE III - MEMBERSHIP

Section 1. Membership Organization. The corporation shall be a membership organization.

Section 2. Qualification of Membership. Any reputable individual of any adult age who likes to sing, regardless of economic status, as no obligation such as dues or audition is mandatory, may be a member of the Beaver Valley Choral Society. All officers, directors and committee members, as well as persons assuming other responsibilities, shall also be members of the Society. A member in good standing is designated as having participated in the Choral Society for a minimum of five (5) previous consecutive years, but not necessarily the immediate prior consecutive five (5) years.

Section 3. Dues. There shall be a voluntary membership fee suggested of all members in the amount of $10 (ten dollars) per concert season. The amount to be determined by the Board of Directors as subject to necessity.

Section 4. Removal of Members. For any or no reason including illegal activity, or disruption of the membership on a regular basis, members may be removed at any time by a majority vote of all members present at a duly called and convened meeting of the membership.

Section 5. Authority of Membership. The membership of the Corporation shall have all rights, powers and privileges conferred upon members of nonprofit corporations by the Nonprofit Corporation Law of 1988, 15 Pa.C.S.A. ยง5101 et seq.

Section 6. Annual Meetings. The Annual Meeting of the membership for the election of directors and the transaction of such other business as may properly come before the meeting shall be held at such time and place as the President, the Chairman of the Board of Directors, the Board or the membership of the Corporation may designate in the month of April before each new fiscal year. The purpose of the meeting will be to elect vacancies to the Board of Directors, disburse funds to pay any indebtedness and transact business as is necessary. The Annual Meeting shall be conducted according to Roberts Rules of Order.

Section 7. Special Meetings. Special Meetings of the membership may be called at any time by the President, the Chairman of the Board of Directors, the Board or the membership according to the guidelines established by the Nonprofit Corporation Law of 1988. Upon written request of any person or persons who shall have duly called a special meeting, it shall be the duty of the Secretary, subject to the minimum notice requirements set forth in these By-laws, to affix the time and place of the meeting, to be held not more than sixty (60) days after the receipt of the request.

Section 8. Notice. Written notice of the Annual Meeting and of any special meeting shall be mailed by the Secretary at least five (5) days before the meeting to each member of record entitled to vote at the meeting at his/her address printed on the books of the Corporation or supplied by him/her to the Corporation for the purpose of notice. The written notice shall state the time and place of the meeting. Written notice of a special meeting shall state the business to be transacted during such special meeting.

Section 9. Consent Action. Any action that may be taken in a meeting of the membership may be taken without a meeting if consents in writing, setting forth the action so taken, shall be signed by all the members, and shall be filed with the Secretary of the Corporation.

Section 10. Quorum. A quorum of a meeting of the membership shall consist of one-third (1/3) of the members of record entitled to vote at any meeting of the membership. The acts of a super-majority of the members present at a meeting at which a quorum is present shall constitute the acts of the membership.

ARTICLE IV - BOARD OF DIRECTORS

Section 1. General. The affairs of the Corporation shall be managed by a Board of Directors, which shall have general charge of the funds and property of the Corporation and may exercise, in addition to the powers conferred upon them by these By-laws, all powers of the Corporation other than those that statute, the Articles or the By-laws required be performed by the members. All officers, employees and agents shall be subject to the control, direction of and removal by the Board.

Section 2. Number.

    a) There shall be seven (7) members of the Board of Directors. Each board member shall serve for six (6) years. However, the first seven (7) board members' terms shall be designated to serve as follows:

    b) Three (3) of the original seven (7) board members shall serve six (6) years, commencing June 1, 2008 through May 31, 2014. All three (3) board members shall be elected by the membership of the Society.

    c) Two (2) of the original seven (7) board members shall serve four (4) years, commencing June 1, 2008 through May 31, 2012. The two (2) board members shall be elected by the membership of the Society.

    d) Two (2) of the original seven (7) board members shall serve two (2) years, commencing June 1, 2008 through May 31, 2010. Both board members shall be elected by the membership of the Society.

Section 3. Initial Board of Directors. The initial Board of Directors shall consist of the following individuals, who shall serve until the first annual meeting of the Board and until their successors are duly elected and qualified.

After the filing of the articles of incorporation, an organizational meeting of the initial Directors shall be held for the purpose of adopting by-laws, electing officers and transacting any other business as may come before the meeting.

Section 4. Appointment Term and Election. At the first annual meeting of the Corporation and at each annual meeting thereafter, the membership shall elect individuals to fill existing vacancies on the Board of Directors for a six (6) year term. If a vacancy occurs on the Board between annual meetings, a special election may be held at a duly called special meeting of the membership to fill the remainder of the term of any vacant position. Directors may serve an unlimited number of consecutive terms. The membership shall select directors from the membership at large.

Section 5. Nominations. Nomination for board members for new terms of office shall be made by the Nominating Committee of the Board, or by petition of the membership signed by at least five (5) members of the Society who are members in good standing at the time of such nomination, or by nomination from the floor at the annual meeting. Such nominations shall be solicited during the second concert season of the Society's fiscal year by the Nominating Committee. Nominations shall be returned to the Society Nominating Committee by March 15th of the election year. Ballots will be distributed to the members of the Choral Society by the Nominating Committee and tabulated before April 30th of the election year. The Nominating Committee shall certify to the full board the names of the persons receiving the greatest number of votes. Such persons shall take office commencing June 1st of the following fiscal year. No person shall be nominated or elected to the Board who is not a member in good standing of the Society.

Section 6. Resignation or Removal. An elected director may resign at any time by instrument in writing to that effect, filed with the Secretary of the Board. An elected Director shall be removed if said Director fails to attend three (3) consecutive duly called and constituted meetings. Whenever any vacancies shall occur among the Directors, the remaining Directors shall constitute the Board of Directors until such vacancy is filled. The remaining Directors may, by a vote of a majority of their number, fill any vacancy in elected Directors for the unexpired term, provided that no change in the number of designated Directors is made.

ARTICLE V - MEETING OF DIRECTORS

Section 1. Fiscal Year. The fiscal year of the Beaver Valley Choral Society shall be from June 1 to May 31 of consecutive years.

Section 2. Meetings. There shall be two (2) regular meetings each year of the Board of Directors of the Society. Said meetings shall be held during the months of May and November. The President of the Society shall select a date, time and place for said meeting and shall notify each Director in writing at least two (2) weeks in advance of each meeting. The agenda of said meeting shall include a review of an annual financial report and an agenda item to disburse funds as needed. The Board of Directors shall also set the annual operating budget at that meeting if it had not been adopted at any prior meeting. This meeting may include such other business as the Board deems advisable. The November meeting shall be designated to disburse the scholarship award(s), to select a Nominating Committee for the purpose of preparing for the forthcoming election of Board vacancies to be conducted during the April Annual Meeting of the Society and to address any other business that has come before the Board of Directors.

Section 3. Special Meetings. Special meetings shall be held when the President deems it advisable or when called by at least three (3) members of the Board of Directors. Notice of the special meeting shall be made in writing to each board member by the President or board members calling said meeting. Said notice shall specify the purpose or purposes of the meeting and contain the date, time and place of said meeting and shall be mailed at least two (2) weeks prior to the date of the meeting.

Section 4. Artistic Director. The Board of Directors will be responsible for the hiring or dismissal of the Artistic Director of the Beaver Valley Choral Society. The Artistic Director's salary shall be determined by the Board of Directors at its regular meeting in May. The Artistic Director may waive his/her salary or donate the entire amount or percentage thereof to the Beaver Valley Choral Society.

Section 5. Roberts Rules of Order. All meetings of the Board of Directors shall be conducted according to Roberts Rules of Order.

Section 6. Quorum. At any regular duly constituted meeting, a quorum shall be defined as the number of Directors present provided that there are in excess of five (5) Directors in attendance.

Section 7. Action by Unanimous Written Consent. If and when the Directors shall severally or collectively consent in writing to any action to be taken by the Corporation, such action shall be a valid corporate action as though it had been authorized at a duly called and convened meeting of the Board of Directors.

ARTICLE VI - OFFICERS

Section 1. Designation and Qualification. There shall be four (4) officers of the Society. Such officers shall be President, Vice-President, Treasurer and Secretary. The President, Vice-President, Treasurer and Secretary shall be elected by a majority of the Board of Directors at the initial organizational meeting and subsequently by the Board at its regular meeting before the end of the fiscal year and shall serve without compensation. They shall be members in good standing of the Society. Officers elected by the initial Board at its organizational meeting shall serve until the Directors' organizational meeting following the next annual meeting of the Corporation. Otherwise, officers shall serve for a term of one (1) year or until their successors have been duly elected and qualified. In the case of any vacancy occurring in any of the said offices, a successor may be elected at any regular meeting of the Board to complete the unexpired term of the vacant office. The Board may name one or more assistant officers, who shall hold their offices for such term and shall have the authority to perform such duties as the Board shall prescribe from time to time. Without exception, any individual may simultaneously serve in one or more offices. In order to be qualified as an officer of the Corporation, an individual must also be a member in good standing of the Board; provided, however, that any person with a position as an officer and a director of the Corporation shall, and shall be expected to, discharge separate fiduciary duties to the Corporation in accordance with each position. In the event that an officer loses his/her position on the Board, he/she shall simultaneously cease to be an officer of the Corporation.

Section 2. President. The President shall be the Chief Executive Officer of the Corporation. The Board of Directors shall select and retain the President under such terms and conditions as it shall provide. The President shall have all authority and responsibility necessary to operate the Corporation in all its activities and departments, subject only to such policies as shall be established by the Board. It shall be the duty of the President to preside over all meetings of the Board of Directors and officers. He/she shall issue the call for all required meetings and any special meeting which may be deemed necessary. The President shall initiate and execute programs and policies that are adopted pursuant to the purposes of the Society. The President shall appoint Board members to all committees and shall oversee the operations of such committees. The President shall direct and administer all business affairs and see that an accurate and complete record is made of all transactions; attend all meetings of the Board; prepare an annual budget with the assistance of the Treasurer showing the expected receipts and expenses as required by the Board; sign any deeds, mortgages, bonds, contracts or other instruments which the Board has authority to execute and has agreed to execute except in the case where the execution shall have been expressly delegated by the Board or these By-laws or by statute to some other officer or agent of the Corporation; chair all meetings of the Board as a member of the Board; and perform all duties incident to the office of the President and such other duties as may from time to time be prescribed by the Board; provided, however, that the custodial and ministerial duties of the office may be delegated to assistant officers or other employees or agents of the Corporation.

Section 3. Vice-President. The Vice-President shall serve as Chief Executive Officer of the Society in the absence of the President. The Vice-President shall perform such other duties as directed by the President or the Board of Directors.

Section 4. Secretary. It shall be the duty of the Secretary to:

    a) Attend all meetings of the Board of Directors and the membership and, when required, meetings of Board committees, to record all votes and the minutes of such transactions in a book to be kept for such purpose and to maintain such records, a copy of the Corporation's Articles of Incorporation and these By-laws at the registered office of the Corporation;

    b) Be custodian of the records and of the seal of the Corporation and see that the seal is affixed to such documents as may be necessary or advisable;

    c) Be custodian of the contracts, policies, leases, deeds and other indicia of title and all other original business records of the Corporation (except tax matters, financial documents and accounting records);

    d) Provide notice of meetings of the Board and the membership; and

    e) Perform all duties incident to the office of the Secretary and such other duties as may from time to time be prescribed by the Board or the President; provided, however, that the custodial and ministerial duties of the office may be delegated to assistant officers or other employees or agents of the Corporation.
Section 5. Treasurer. It shall be the duty of the Treasurer to:

    a) See that the lists, books, reports, statements, tax returns, certificates and other documents and records required by law are properly prepared, kept and filed;

    b) Be the principal officer in charge of tax and financial matters;

    c) Have charge and custody of and be responsible for the funds, securities and investments of the Corporation and to disburse the funds as ordered by the Board of Directors;

    d) Render to the President and the Board whenever they may require it an account of all the transactions as Treasurer and report as to the financial position and operations of the Corporation; and

    e) Perform all duties incident to the office of the Treasurer and such other duties as may from time to time be prescribed by the Board or the President; provided, however, that the custodial and ministerial duties of the office may be delegated to assistant officers or other employees or agents of the Corporation.

Section 6. Resignation and Removal. Any officer may resign at any time by giving notice in writing to the President or the Board of Directors. Such resignation shall take effect at the time specified therein and, unless otherwise specified therein, the acceptance of such resignation shall not be necessary to make it effective. Except as the Board may otherwise agree in writing, the Board shall have the right at any time to remove one or more of the officers of the Corporation, with or without cause.

Section 7. Assistant Officers. The Board of Directors may from time to time appoint assistant officers of the Corporation to perform such duties and have such responsibilities as the Board may assign to them.

ARTICLE VII - COMMITTEES

Section 1. The committees of the Board of Directors shall be as follows: Nominating Committee, Rules Committee, Finance Committee, Membership Committee, Music Committee, Scholarship Committee and Social Committee. The Board of Directors may create additional committees as they, in their sound discretion, deem advisable. The Board shall designate the members and the chairman of each committee. Each committee shall keep minutes of its proceedings and shall report its actions to the Board.

Section 2. The committees shall consist of three (3) persons selected by the President, subject to the right of the Board of Directors to overrule the President. The committee members may be selected from the Board of Directors or other members in good standing of the Beaver Valley Choral Society.

Section 3. Scholarship Committee.

    a) The Scholarship Committee of the Choral Society Board of Directors will function to select the recipient(s) of "The Philip H. Inman Excellence in Choral Conducting Arts Scholarship" awards. These awards are to be given to music students, as recommended by and selected by the Scholarship Committee. The criteria for selection will be determined by the Scholarship Committee with guidance from the Choral Society Board of Directors. The names(s) of the recipient(s) will be given to the Board of Directors of the Beaver Valley Choral Society for approval and dissemination of funds.

    b) The guidelines for scholarship recipient(s) will be as follows: Each year, the Beaver Valley Choral Society Board of Directors Scholarship Committee will select from the following colleges or universities in rotating order (Carnegie Mellon University, Duquesne University, Grove City College, Geneva College and Westminster College) a qualifying applicant(s) for a scholarship award. If there would be no qualifying candidate from the schools listed, the Scholarship Committee could select a recipient from another accredited college, university or school for the performing musical arts. The Beaver Valley Choral Society scholarship award applicant(s) must be an undergraduate or graduate student enrolled in a post- high school music program with an interest in Choral Studies. The scholarship amounts will be determined on a yearly basis and given at the discretion of the Board of Directors of the Beaver Valley Choral Society in accordance with the guidelines of the Internal Revenue Service. The award(s) will be in the amount of no less than $500.00 per recipient(s).

    The scholarship(s) will be awarded on the basis of the following criteria:
      1.) Scholastic achievement in which the applicant must have maintained a 3.5 QPA or higher;
      2.) Achieved Junior year or higher;
      3.) Major in music, focus studies on choral conducting arts;
      4.) Recipient (s) residency requirements - Order of consideration may be as follows:
        a. Resident of Beaver County, Pennsylvania
        b. Resident of contiguous counties in Pennsylvania
        c. Resident of Western Pennsylvania
        d. Resident of Pennsylvania
        e. Any student as deemed deserving of the award by the Beaver Valley Choral Society Board of Directors.
    c) Guideline requirements for the Dean of Music or Department Head of the selected college or university shall be as follows:
      1.) Conduct the search for and select appropriate scholarship nominee(s) based on scholarship award criteria as stated in Section 3 of Article VII;

      2.) Provide declaration of, grade and competency levels of nominee(s);

      3.) Schedule meeting with the Beaver Valley Choral Society Scholarship Committee to present prospective eligible nominee(s) and provide letter of justification on school letterhead addressed to Choral Society Scholarship Committee Chairperson.
        a. Letter must contain the title and signature of its author;
        b. Letter must contain the title and signature of student's choral/department head/vocal professor.
    d) Scholarship Award Recipient(s) Requirements will be as follows:
      1.) Student recipient must forward a letter of acknowledgement to Mr. Philip H. Inman or, if by necessity, to his family and a copy to his successor as Beaver Valley Choral Society Artistic Director within three (3) weeks of notification of the scholarship award and include a brief description of his/her goals relating to the study and practice in the area of choral arts;

      2.) The recipient is invited to attend a concert of the Beaver Valley Choral Society as selected by the President of the Board of Directors, to be recognized for the scholarship.
    e) The scholarship award funds will be delivered to the Dean of Music or Department Head in the name of the scholarship recipient and must be immediately applied directly to related choral arts studies expenses of the student(s). A school not in adherence to the directive of the scholarship mandates will be eliminated from future consideration.

    f) The amount of the scholarship awards will be determined each fiscal year by the Beaver Valley Choral Society Board of Directors at the first regular Board meeting in November of each year.

    g) There shall be a separate Scholarship Trust Account for the purpose of scholarships given from within the Beaver Valley Choral Society set apart from all other accounts. From this account funds will be drawn for the scholarship award(s) each year as determined by the Board of Directors of the Beaver Valley Choral Society.

Section 4. Special Committees. Special Committees may be appointed for such special task(s) as circumstances warrant. A Special Committee shall limit its activities to the accomplishments of the task(s) for which it is appointed and shall have no power to act except as specifically conferred by the Board of Directors. Upon completion of the task(s) for which it was appointed, such Special Committee shall stand dissolved.

Section 5. Resignation and Removal. Any member of a Special or Standing committee may resign at any time by giving written notice to the chairman of the committee or the Board of Directors. Such resignation, which may or may not be made contingent on formal acceptance, takes effect on the date of receipt or at any specified later time specified in it. Any member of the committee, except an ex-officio member, may be removed at any time by a resolution adopted by a majority of the Directors then in office. Any ex-officio member of a committee shall cease to be such if he/she ceases to hold a designated position which is the basis of ex-officio membership.

Section 6. Vacancies. A vacancy on any committee and any increase in the membership thereto shall be filled for the unexpired portion of the term by the Board of Directors.

Section 7. Meetings and Notices. Meetings of a committee may be called by the Board of Directors or the chairman of the committee. Each committee shall meet as often as is necessary to perform its duties, but no less frequently than annually. Oral or written notice of the time and place of any meeting of a committee, except in an emergency, shall be given at least twenty-four (24) hours prior to the meeting.

Section 8. Manner of Acting. Unless otherwise specified herein, the act of a majority of the members of a committee present at a meeting shall constitute the act of the committee so meeting.

ARTICLE VIII - FISCAL YEAR

Section 1. Receipts and Disbursements. The fiscal year of the Corporation shall commence June 1 and end May 31. At the end of each fiscal year, the Treasurer shall submit to the Board of Directors a full detailed report of the receipts and disbursements during the preceding year.

Section 2. Annual Report. The Board of Directors shall annually present to the membership a report, verified by the President and Treasurer or by a majority of the Board, showing, in appropriate detail, the following:

    a) The assets and liabilities, including trust funds, of the Corporation at the end of the fiscal year which just concluded;

    b) The principal changes in assets and liabilities, including trust funds, during the fiscal year which just concluded;

    c) The revenue or receipts of the Corporation, both unrestricted and restricted to particular purposes, for the fiscal year which just concluded, including separate data with respect to each trust fund held by or for the Corporation;

    d) The expenses or disbursements of the Corporation, for both general and restricted purposes, during the fiscal year which just concluded, including separate data with respect to each trust fund held by or for the Corporation; and

    e) The number of members of the Corporation as of the date of the report together with a statement of increase or decrease in such number during the fiscal year which just concluded and a statement of where the names and addresses of the current members may be found. The annual report shall be filed with the minutes of the meetings of members.

Section 3. Annual Audit. At the discretion of the Board of Directors, all the accounts and books together with all supporting data may be audited at the end of each fiscal year by a firm of disinterested certified public accountants employed by the Board.

Section 4. Records. The Corporation shall maintain complete books or records of account.

ARTICLE IX - LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION OF DIRECTORS, OFFICERS AND OTHER PERSONS

Section 1. Personal Liability of Directors. No Director of the Corporation shall be personally liable for monetary damages as such for any action or failure to act, provided, however, that this provision shall not eliminate liability:

    a) For (1) any breach or failure to perform the duties of his/her office under Section 5713 of the Nonprofit Corporation Law of 1988, 15 Pa.C.S.A. ยง5713 (as those duties may be) modified by Section 8332 of the Pennsylvania Judicial Code, 42 Pa.C.S. ยง8332, and (2) where such breach or failure to perform such duties constitutes self- dealing, willful misconduct or recklessness;

    b) Pursuant to criminal statute; or

    c) For the payment of taxes pursuant to local, state or federal law.


Section 2. Indemnification; Actions Other Than Those By or in the Right of the Corporation. The Corporation shall indemnify any person who was or is a party or is threatened to be made a party to any threatened, pending or completed action, suit or proceeding, whether civil, criminal, administrative or investigative (other than an action by or in the right of this Corporation), by reason of the fact that he/she is or was a Director or officer of this Corporation, or is or was serving at the request of this Corporation as a Director or officer of another corporation, partnership, joint venture, trust or other enterprise against expenses (including attorneys' fees), judgments, fines and amounts paid in settlement actually and reasonably incurred by him/her in connection with such action, suit or proceeding if the Director or officer acted in good faith and in a manner he/she reasonably believed to be in, or not opposed to, the best interests of this Corporation, and with respect to any criminal action or proceeding, had no reasonable cause to believe his/her conduct was unlawful. The termination of any action, suit, or preceding by judgment, order, settlement, conviction, or upon a plea of nolo contendere or its equivalent shall not, of itself, create a presumption that a Director or an officer did not act in good faith and in a manner which he/she reasonable believed to be in, or not opposed to, the best interests of this Corporation, and with respect to any criminal action or proceeding, had reasonable cause to believe that his/her conduct was unlawful.

Section 3. Indemnification; Actions By or in the Right of the Corporation. The Corporation shall indemnify any person who was or is a party or is threatened to be made a party to any threatened, pending or completed action or suit by or in the right of this Corporation to procure a judgment in its favor by reason of the fact that he/she is or was a Director or officer of this Corporation or is or was serving at the request of this Corporation as a Director or officer of another corporation, partnership, joint venture, trust or other enterprise against expenses (including attorneys' fees) actually and reasonably incurred by him/her in connection with the defense or settlement of such action or suit if the Director or officer acted in good faith and in a manner he/she reasonably believed to be in, or not opposed to, the best interests of this Corporation.

Section 4. Determination Required. Any indemnification under this Article IX shall be made by a determination that the indemnification of the Director or officer is proper in the circumstances because he/she has met the applicable standard of conduct set forth in such subsection. Such determination shall be made:

    a) By the Board of Directors by a majority vote of a quorum consisting of Directors who were not parties to such action, suit or proceeding;

    b) If such a quorum is not obtainable, or even if obtainable, a majority vote of a quorum of disinterested Directors so directs, by independent legal counsel in a written opinion; or

    c) By the membership.


Section 5. Advance of Expenses. Expenses incurred in defending a civil or criminal action, suit or proceeding may be paid by the Corporation in advance of the final disposition of such action, suit or proceeding as authorized by the Board of Directors upon receipt of an undertaking by or on behalf of the Director or officer to repay such amount unless it shall ultimately be determined that he/she is entitled to be indemnified by this Corporation as authorized in this Article IX.

Section 6. Nonexclusivity; Duration. The indemnification provided by this Article IX shall not be deemed exclusive of any other rights to which those seeking indemnification may be entitled under any agreement, vote of disinterested Directors or otherwise, both as to action in their official capacity and as to action in another capacity while holding such office and shall continue as to a person who has ceased to be a Director or officer and shall inure to the benefit of the heirs, executors and administrators of such person.

Section 7. Insurance. This Corporation shall have power to purchase and maintain insurance on behalf of any person who is or was a Director or officer of the Corporation or is or was serving at the request of this Corporation as a Director or officer of another corporation, partnership, joint venture, trust or other enterprise against any liability asserted against him/her and incurred by him/her in any such capacity, or arising out of status as such, whether or not this Corporation would have the power to indemnify him/her against such liability under the provisions of this Article IX.

Section 8. Applicability to Other Persons. The provisions of this Article IX may be applicable to persons who are not Directors or officers of the Corporation and such persons may be similarly indemnified for service by or on behalf of the Corporation to the extent authorized by any resolution of the Board of Directors.

ARTICLE X - NOTICE

Section 1. Manner. Whenever written notice is required to be given to any person, it may be given to such person personally, by sending a copy thereof by first class mail, postage prepaid, or by e-mail, to his/her address appearing on the books of the Corporation or, in the case of Directors, supplied by him/her to the Corporation for the purpose of notice. If notice is transmitted by mail or e-mail, it shall be deemed to have been given when deposited in the United States mail or through Internet for transmission to such person. A notice of a meeting shall include the time and place of the meeting and any other information required to be given by these By-laws.

Section 2. Waiver. Whenever any notice is required to be given under the provisions of the Nonprofit Corporation Law of 1988 or the provisions of the Corporation's Articles or these By-laws, a waiver thereof, in writing, signed by the person or persons entitled to such notice, whether before or after the time stated therein, shall be deemed equivalent to the giving of such notice.

ARTICLE XI - GENERAL PROVISIONS

Section 1. Offices. The address of the registered office of the Corporation shall be 326 Jackson Street, Rochester, Pennsylvania 15074. The Corporation may have such other offices at such places within or without the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania as the business of the Corporation may require.

Section 2. Seal. The Board shall prescribe the form of a corporate seal.

ARTICLE XII - DISSOLUTION

Section 1. If for any reason the Society shall terminate or should be required to dissolve, all funds in the treasury of the Society shall be distributed to the Choral Department of an accredited college or university as determined by the Board of Directors of the disbanding Beaver Valley Choral Society. It is the desire of the Society that these transferred funds be used to perpetuate "The Philip H. Inman Excellence in Choral Conducting Arts Scholarship" based on the same criteria as set forth in Article 7, Section 3. Any other property, real, personal or mixed, shall be transferred in the same manner to perpetuate the aforementioned scholarship based on the same criteria as set forth in Article 7, Section 3 of these By-laws or for one or more other exempt purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, or the corresponding section of any future federal tax code, or shall be distributed to the federal government, or to a state or other local government for a public purpose.

ARTICLE XIII - AMENDMENTS TO BY-LAWS

Section 1. Amendments to these By-laws will only be effective after adoption of the same by 2/3 super majority of the membership present in good standing of the Beaver Valley Choral Society at two (2) successive regular or special meetings thereof called for that purpose.
